Professional Summary

AI researcher with over 10 years of experience across finance, biology, mathematics, physics, and artificial intelligence, spanning academic research and production systems. My work combines machine learning, causal modeling, and complexity science to study how intelligent behavior emerges under constraints. I have conducted research on network reduction and causal information replication in physical and computational systems, focusing on reducing time, space, and computational resources while preserving explanatory or functional structure. Alongside applied work in quantitative trading and cloud-scale optimization, I have investigated the strengths and limits of large language models and agentic systems using complexity-based methods. My research has been published in peer-reviewed venues in collaboration with Oxford University and Oxford Immune Algorithmics. I currently serve as AI Engineer Lead at K-Square Group, integrating research insights into real-world AI systems.
